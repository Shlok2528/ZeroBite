Saving food is a key part of reducing food waste and building a more sustainable food system. It focuses on preventing food from being wasted in the first place, which is the most effective and environmentally friendly strategy.

Methods applicable in our daily Life

1) Smart Planning and Shopping
  1. Plan meals for the week and make shopping lists based on what you already have.
  2. impulse buying and stick to what you realistically need.
  3. Buy perishables in smaller quantities to avoid spoilage.
2) Proper Storage Techniques
  1. Learn where foods last longest.
  2. Use airtight containers to extend shelf life.
  3. Understand food labels—“best before” usually refers to quality, not safety.
3) Creative Cooking
  1. Use leftovers to create new dishes like soups, stews, or stir-fries.
  2. Repurpose ingredients (e.g., overripe fruits for smoothies or baking).
  3. Practice portion control when cooking and serving food.
4) Freezing and Preserving
  1. Freeze extra meals, fruits, vegetables, and bread to extend their life.
  2. Try preservation methods like pickling, canning, or drying.
5) Use Technology
  1. Apps can help track pantry items, suggest recipes based on leftovers, or connect stores with consumers to sell surplus food at reduced prices.
